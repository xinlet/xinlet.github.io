Intro

Xinle Tian is currently a PhD student in Statistics at University of Bath (Department of Mathematical Sciences)

Topic: Development of low-rank and sparse statistical modelling techniques with public health applications

Supervisors: Sandipan Roy and Matt Nunes

Eduation:

2021 -- present PhD in statistics at University of Bath, Bath, UK

2019 -- 2021 MS in Statistics at The George Washington University, Washington DC, USA

2015 -- 2019 BSc in Mathematics at The University of British Columbia, Vancouver, Canada

Work

Project 1: Multi-response linear regression estimation based on low-rank pre-smoothing

We establish theoretical results on the performance of the proposed methodology, and quantify its benefit empirically in a number of simulated experiments. We also demonstrate our proposed low-rank pre-smoothing technique on real data arising from the environmental sciences.

About Me

In addition to my focus on scientific studies, I've always harboured a deep passion for the arts. Photography, in particular, has become a cherished hobby of mine, stemming from my early experiences with an old Canon camera. Through the lens, I'm able to seize the beauty in the smallest details of our lives.
